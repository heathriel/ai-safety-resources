# Attributions and Credits

This repository curates and builds upon the work of many organizations and researchers in the AI safety and ethics community. We gratefully acknowledge the following sources.

## Frameworks and Methodologies

- **TradewindAI Responsible AI Toolkit**: The structure and content of `best-practices/responsible_ai_toolkit.md` draws heavily from TradewindAI's RAI Toolkit framework, which provides a centralized process for aligning AI projects with responsible AI best practices and the DoD AI Ethical Principles.  
  [https://rai.tradewindai.com/](https://rai.tradewindai.com/)

- **IBM AI Fairness 360 (AIF360)**: The bias detection demo in `evaluation-scripts/bias_detection_demo.ipynb` uses the AIF360 open-source toolkit developed by IBM Research.  
  [https://aif360.res.ibm.com/](https://aif360.res.ibm.com/)

- **NIST AI Risk Management Framework**: Referenced throughout this repository. Published by the National Institute of Standards and Technology.  
  [https://airc.nist.gov/AI_RMF_Knowledge_Base/Playbook](https://airc.nist.gov/AI_RMF_Knowledge_Base/Playbook)

## Adversarial Testing Methodologies

- **Anthropic AI Safety Research**: Adversarial testing approaches in `evaluation-scripts/prompt_adversary_test.ipynb` are informed by Anthropic's red-teaming and AI safety research practices.  
  [https://www.anthropic.com/research](https://www.anthropic.com/research)

- **OpenAI Red-Teaming Practices**: Additional inspiration for adversarial prompt testing methodologies.
